tacofancy

Tastacular.

There is a whole world of foods that are similar, but aren't exactly tacos. This directory is dedicated to them.

From @maxogden's gist: tacos are a category of food that are relatively small in size and are served on a open platform of thin carbohydrate filled material. things that make you want to sleep after eating or are sealed off when served are not tacos (see instead: burritos, pizza)

  • dosa
  • roti
  • peking duck which is usually served with mushu pancakes
  • gua bao
  • smørbrød
  • lefse
  • falafel and pita
  • arepas - note: omit top half when serving for proper taco categorization
  • really small pizzas - if you make large pizzas they make you want to go to sleep after eating one which breaks the first rule of tacos
  • injera - make sure to subdivide into smaller diameter pieces
  • small crepes - again the key is the size. also what is the different between a blintz and a crepe?
  • sushi - as long as it is not served in complete rolls sushi is a taco. when served in rolls it is known as a sushirrito
  • sopes and other antojitos
  • tacos tacos are tacos as long as they 1) come from trucks in oakland or 2) are made by hand in front of you from masa flour

not tacos:

  • hot dogs - not thin enough
  • doner kebab - too heavy
